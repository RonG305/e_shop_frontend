Over-the-Counter (OTC) Medications:
Includes common medications that can be purchased without a prescription, such as pain relievers, antihistamines, cough and cold medicines, and antacids.


Prescription Medications:
Includes medications that require a prescription from a healthcare provider, such as antibiotics, antidepressants, blood pressure medications, and insulin.


Dermatological Products:
Includes skincare products like creams, ointments, and lotions for conditions such as acne, eczema, psoriasis, and fungal infections.
Vitamins and Supplements:

Includes dietary supplements like vitamins, minerals, herbal extracts, and probiotics that are used to supplement the diet and promote health.

First Aid Supplies:
Includes bandages, gauze, antiseptics, adhesive tape, and other supplies used for treating minor injuries and emergencies.

Personal Care Products:
Includes hygiene products like soaps, shampoos, toothpaste, deodorants, and feminine hygiene products.


Baby and Infant Care Products:
Includes products specifically designed for babies and infants, such as baby formula, diapers, baby wipes, and teething gels.


Medical Devices and Equipment:
Includes devices and equipment used for medical purposes, such as blood glucose monitors, inhalers, nebulizers, and mobility aids.


Pain Management Products:
Includes products for managing pain, such as analgesic creams, hot/cold packs, muscle relaxants, and transcutaneous electrical nerve stimulation (TENS) devices.


Digestive Health Products:
Includes products for digestive health, such as antacids, laxatives, probiotics, and digestive enzymes.
